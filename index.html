The Americans with Disabilities Act was passed in 1990. It is a civil rights law that prohibits discrimination on the basis of disability. The law imposes accessibility requirements on public accommodations, including public toilets.

In New York City, there are 616 public toilets in public parks across five boroughs and only 129 are handicap accessible. In Brooklyn, there are 142 public toilets in public parks-the most of any borough-but the most number of handicap accessbile public toilets are in Manhattan, a total of 29.

The least number of handicap accessible public toilets in public parks are in Staten Island, but Staten Island also has the least number of public parks. It is also the only borough with fewer public parks than handicap accessible public toilets-18 parks with 20 handicap accessible public toilets.

This scatterplot attempts to show a correlation between handicap accessible toilets in public parks and neighborhood wealth.

The Upper Westside has the greatest number of handicap accessible toilets in its public parks, with 11 handicap accessible toilets. The median income of the area is $117,421.

By comparison, Lower Manhattan (PUMA 3810) has the highest median income of $140,213, but the least number of handicap accessible toilets. Lower Manhattan has only one handicap accessible toilet.

The South Bronx (PUMA 3710) has the lowest median income of $24,849 and is thus the least wealthiest neighborhood in New York City. However, it has 4 handicap accessible toilets - 2 at St. Mary's Park and Recreation Center, 1 at Millbrook Playground and 1 at Captain Rivera Playground.

The concentration of handicap accessible toilets in the Upper Westside can be attributed to the fact that many of these toilets are in Central Park, the fifth-largest urban park in New York City and the most visited park in the United States of America. Both the Upper Westside and Central Park belong to the same Public Use Microdata Area.
